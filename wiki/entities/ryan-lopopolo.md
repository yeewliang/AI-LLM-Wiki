# Ryan Lopopolo

## Overview
OpenAI engineer credited with an influential February 2026 account of a small team shipping at high volume by adopting [[harness-engineering]] rather than relying on better models or prompts.

## Why this entity matters to AI
His case study is the empirical anchor for the [[harness-engineering]] argument: a 3-person team produced ~1,000,000 lines of code and 1,500+ pull requests over 5 months — "no special model, no secret prompt; they changed their working method." The claim reframes production-codebase maintainability problems as a question of *environment design* (the constraint system and [[agent-harness]]) rather than prompt skill, and is cited as evidence that the engineer's output shifts from code to constraints.

## Key works / outputs
- February 2026 article on the team's working method and output volume — the source of the ~1M LOC / 1,500+ PR figures (reported, not independently verified in this wiki).

## Affiliations and relationships
- Engineer at OpenAI.

## Current status / latest developments
As of mid-2026 the case study circulates as a canonical example motivating [[harness-engineering]]. The specific productivity figures are widely cited but originate from a single first-party account; treat as illustrative rather than benchmarked.
