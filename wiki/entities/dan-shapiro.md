# Dan Shapiro

## Overview
Author of the [[five-levels-of-ai-engineering]] framework — a staged model (spicy autocomplete → AI coding assistants → autonomous agents → collaborative agent networks → software factory) used to situate teams along the AI-in-software trajectory and match governance to capability.

## Why this entity matters to AI
The five-level framework has become a common reference for organisational AI strategy, used (e.g.) by [[govtech-singapore]] to structure its public-sector adoption plan. Its primary contribution is preventing the trap of treating AI adoption as a single dimension to maximise: instead, levels are matched per system class, with low-risk systems racing ahead and critical infrastructure deliberately staying at lower autonomy with stronger guardrails.

## Key works / outputs
- The five-level model for AI in software development — used as a planning lens across multiple organisations and as the structuring spine of GovTech Singapore's strategy
