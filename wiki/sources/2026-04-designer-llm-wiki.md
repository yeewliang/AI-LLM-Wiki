# The Designer's LLM Wiki

## Core argument
The [[llm-wiki-pattern]] is particularly valuable for designers, who generate large amounts of insight across fragmented tools (Figma, Miro, Notion, Slack) with no accumulation mechanism. The pattern reframes the practitioner's role from librarian to curator. The article frames the distinction as "knowledge accumulation vs. knowledge capture."

## Key concepts introduced or used
- [[llm-wiki-pattern]] — Framed specifically for design practice; step-by-step setup for non-terminal users using Claude chat interface and [[obsidian]] desktop
- [[memex]] — Cited as the intellectual origin; the LLM Wiki described as "closer to what [Bush] imagined than anything we've built in 80 years"; the key unsolved problem Bush faced (maintenance) is resolved by the LLM
- [[obsidian]] — Canvas feature highlighted as particularly valuable for visual thinkers; spatial knowledge graph as whiteboard analogue

## Notable claims or data points
- Karpathy's `llm-wiki.md` gist: 5,000+ stars and 16 million views within days of posting (April 2026).
- Karpathy's wiki at time of post: ~100 articles, 400,000 words on a single research topic.
- Polgár (product design leader): switched entire note-taking workflow to [[obsidian]] so Claude Code could have direct file access — no MCP complexity, no data leaving the machine, no vendor lock-in.
- "Three phases" framing attributed (without direct quotation) to [[andrej-karpathy]]: (1) vibe coding, (2) agentic engineering, (3) AI managing knowledge. Treat as editorial interpretation until confirmed.
- Most knowledge systems are "landfills" (accumulate without structure); LLM Wiki pattern is framed as "a library that builds itself."
